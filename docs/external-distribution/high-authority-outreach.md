## TIER 1 — most realistic high-value paths (start here)

### A. "Expert source" platforms (HARO successors) — BEST ROI for a small company
Journalists post queries; you answer; if used, you get quoted with a link from a real publication
(often high authority). Free.
- **Featured.com** (formerly Terkel) — https://featured.com — answer business/tech expert questions.
- **Qwoted** — https://www.qwoted.com — PR/journalist matching, has tech/manufacturing queries.
- **Help a B2B Writer** — https://helpab2bwriter.com — B2B-focused queries, often electronics/supply-chain.
- **SourceBottle** — https://www.sourcebottle.com — broader, some tech.
> NOTE: HARO/Connectively shut down in 2024–2025; the four above are the current replacements.
- Difficulty: Medium. Effort: 10–20 min per good answer. I draft the answers; you paste/submit.
- What you'd answer: PCB/PCBA sourcing, China manufacturing, supply-chain, prototyping, hardware
  startup advice — things you genuinely know.

### B. Technical content platforms with real authority (dofollow or high-traffic)
Publish a genuinely useful technical piece; link back to huitaipcb.com.
- **Hackster.io** — https://www.hackster.io — hardware community (owned by Avnet), high relevance.
  Create a company profile + publish a build/guide. Strong fit.
- **Medium** (electronics/hardware publications) — high DA, great referral; links usually nofollow
  but worth it for reach + brand. Republish your knowledge articles with canonical to your site.
- **Dev.to** / **Hashnode** — developer/embedded audience; allow a canonical link back to your site.
- **Hackaday.io** (projects, not the blog) — community project pages; relevant audience.
- Difficulty: Low–Medium. I write the article; you create the account + post.

### C. Q&A / community (mostly nofollow, but visibility + referral + brand)
- **Electronics Stack Exchange** — https://electronics.stackexchange.com — answer real PCB/assembly
  questions genuinely; reputation + occasional profile link.
- **Reddit** — r/PrintedCircuitBoard, r/AskElectronics, r/embedded, r/hardware — answer, don't spam.
- **Quora** — answer "China PCB assembly", "how to find a PCBA manufacturer" type questions.
- Difficulty: Low. Rule: be genuinely helpful first; link only when truly relevant.

## TIER 2 — aspirational (revisit in 6–12 months once you have reputation/case studies)

### D. Industry / hardware blogs that accept contributed articles
- **Predictable Designs** (John Teel) — hardware-startup audience; pitch a guest/expert piece.
- EMS / PCB industry blogs and newsletters that accept guest contributions (verify per-site).
- Difficulty: Medium–High. Needs a relationship + a strong, non-promotional article.

### E. Trade press (hard, cold pitches rarely work)
- **All About Circuits**, **EDN**, **EE Times**, **Electronics Weekly**, **Hackaday** (blog).
- Realistic angle: not a vendor ad, but a genuinely newsworthy technical contribution, data, or a
  notable customer project. Long game.

### F. Podcasts (relationship-based)
- **Pick, Place, Podcast** (MacroFab), **The Hardware Entrepreneur**, **Embedded.fm**.
- Angle: offer a specific, useful topic (e.g. "common DFM mistakes that blow up overseas PCBA orders").

### G. Trade associations (authoritative, some paid)
- **IPC** membership (electronics manufacturing standards body) — authoritative member listing; paid.
- Local Shenzhen / Guangdong electronics manufacturing associations.
